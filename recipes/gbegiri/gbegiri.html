Amala and Gbegiri soup

Prep Time:

30 mins

Cook Time

15 mins

Total Time:

35 mins

Servings:

4

Ingredients

  • 2 Cups of Black eyed or Brown Beans
  • 3 Tablesppoos of Palm Oil
  • 2 tablespoons of Crayfish Powder
  • 1 Tablespoon of Locust Beans (Iru)
  • 1-2 tablespoons of Grounded Dried Pepper(Atagungun)
  • 1 Knorr Cude
  • Salt to taste

Directions

  1. Soak some beans and peel. after which, you should add the peeled beans to a pot on high heat.
  2. Add water and cook until it's soft and mushy. To play it safe, start with a little water and add more; remeber to hold off on the salt.
  3. Once it's soft, use an Ijabe (Ewedu broom) to whisk the beans or a whisk. Or let it cool down a little before putting it in a blender; make sure to add a little water before blending until it's smooth.
  4. If you use an ijabe, be sure to pass through a seive to remove clumps for a smooth consistency.
  5. Next, move into a pot on low heat and add very little water to the paste until you get a semi-fluid consistency.
  6. Now add palm oil, Locust Beans, Crayfish Powder, Knorr cube, Ground Pepper and Salt. Make sure add a little Palm Oil, just enough to change the paste's colour.
  7. Cook for 5-7 minutes, occasionally stirring to prevent burning.
  8. Once well thickened and mixed properly with the oil.
  9. Finally, serve with some Amala and Ewedu Soup.
